Advanced Imaging Modalities

Each scan is performed with precision using advanced equipment. Below are brief guides for patients undergoing each modality.

MRI – GE Signa Explorer 1.5T with Breast Coil

What it does: High-resolution imaging of brain, spine, joints, abdomen & breast.

Prep: Usually none. Fasting may be required for abdominal MRI. Inform staff of any implants.

CT Scan – GE Brivo 365

What it does: Fast 3D scans of brain, chest, abdomen, bones, and trauma.

Prep: Fasting 4–6 hours if contrast scan. Let us know about allergies or renal issues.

Ultrasound & Doppler – GE Voluson E10, S8

What it does: Obstetrics, abdomen, pelvis, thyroid, testis, and vascular flow studies.

Prep: Full bladder for pelvic scans. Fasting for upper abdomen.

Liver Elastography / Fat Quantification

What it does: Measures liver stiffness and fat content for conditions like fatty liver and fibrosis.

Prep: 3–4 hours fasting preferred. Non-invasive and quick.

DEXA – Bone & Body Composition

What it does: Assesses bone density and body fat/muscle ratios. Helps in osteoporosis diagnosis and fitness tracking.

Prep: No calcium tablets 24 hours before. Wear light clothing.

Digital X-ray & Mammography

What it does: Chest, limbs, spine, KUB X-rays. Mammography for breast screening.

Prep: No powder or deodorant before mammogram. Remove metallic items.

Dental & ENT CBCT

What it does: Cone beam CT for detailed scan of teeth, jaw, and sinuses.

Prep: No special preparation. Short scan duration.
